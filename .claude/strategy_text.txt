Public data-stack teardowns. Pick a YC or Techstars alum every 2 weeks that has published anything

case: content. Best case: they DM you. 2–3 cases per quarter convert.

Fractional-CFO revenue share. 20 fractional CFOs in the US/UK on LinkedIn. Message each: "I give

Free audit virality. Publish a "Databricks cost audit" that takes a screenshot of their bill and returns
20–40% savings. Most audits will find something. Screenshot of savings → LinkedIn post → more audits
requested. The audit itself is the content.

Goal: Land the first 2–3 paying clients. Validate the offer, hooks, and channel mix with real money.

Top 3 bottlenecks: (1) delivery bottleneck — you're still engineer + salesperson; (2) content burnout at
week 8; (3) quality inconsistency between early clients.

Leading metric: hours billed per client per week. Target: under 10.

Setting up the private agency account (so your colleagues don't notice)
The only reliably invisible LinkedIn setup is an account that isn't indexed by your real identity signals. Here's
the practical checklist:
•
Separate email. Use kuberan@databyt.in (after you set up the domain email). Never reuse any email
your colleagues know.
•
Separate phone. LinkedIn will ask for SMS verification. Use a different SIM, a cheap prepaid number, or a
service like Textverified ($1–3 one-time).
•
Identity framing. LinkedIn's terms require a real name. Use "Kuberan | DataByt" or "Kuberan · Data &
AI for Startups" — your real first name + business tag. This is compliant and doesn't look like a
throwaway.
•
Do not sync email or phone contacts. When LinkedIn asks "find people you know" — skip every time.
This is the #1 way colleagues show up in your "People you may know".
•
Privacy settings: Profile discovery → off for contacts who have your email or phone. "Who can see your
activity" → connections only. Turn off "viewers of this profile also viewed".
•
Distinct photo + banner. Not the headshot from your corporate profile. Reverse-image-searchable
photos will get cross-linked.
•
Work history: list only "Founder · DataByt · 2025–Present". No current employer. No overlapping
companies.
•
Network cleanly from scratch. First 100 connections should be US/UK founders, CTOs, and fractional
CFOs — zero overlap with your current network. LinkedIn's graph will not cross-recommend once the
neighborhood is different.
•
Log in from a different browser (or dedicated profile in Chrome/Firefox). Don't use the same browser as
your personal LinkedIn — cookies bleed.
Honest caveat. No setup is 100% invisible. Shared IP addresses, mutual connections through a
conference, or someone searching your name + "DataByt" can still reveal you. The goal isn't perfect
stealth — it's making sure your account doesn't auto-surface to coworkers through LinkedIn's normal
recommendation loops. That's achievable with the above.
